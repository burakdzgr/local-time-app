What is the Clock?

Clocks, in its simplest definition, are devices that measure time on the basis of criteria set by us humans and show the difference between two times.

The working principle of the watches, which have mechanical and digital varieties, is based on the number counting mechanism. This mechanism is universally based on a day consisting of 24 hours, an hour of 60 minutes, a minute of 60 seconds, and a second of 60 milliseconds. Therefore, whatever the type, the way clocks present time to us necessarily includes several of these units.

Clocks that show time in hours, minutes, seconds and milliseconds are vital for both our work performance and the order of our daily lives. The smart watches that have emerged in recent years are the best proof of how much human beings can improve themselves and a striking example of the convenience that technological progress offers us.

A Brief History of the Watch?

The invention of the clock, BC. It goes back to 4000's. This journey, which started with the invention of the sundial by the Egyptians, is unfortunately interrupted when it gets dark. This is probably why the invention of the sundial was followed by sand and water clocks. However, the production of mechanical watches, as we understand it, reached 1500s.

In 1524, a German locksmith named Peter Henlien produced the first winding watch in history. Then, German, French, Swedish and English mechanical watches began to be produced and over time, watches became important accessories that reflect people's perception of fashion.

In this adventure, which ranged from pendulum clocks to pocket chronometers and then to battery-powered watches, the deviations in seconds in time measurement disappeared with the introduction of electronic watches in 1970. Thus, the watches have become truly robust, punctual and reliable.
